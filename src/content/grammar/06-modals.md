## 情態動詞的共同規則

情態動詞（Modal Verbs）：can / could / may / might / will / would / shall / should / must / ought to

共同規則：
- 後面接**動詞原形**（不加 to，不加 s）
- 本身**不隨主詞變化**（沒有 he mights / she cans）
- 否定：modal + **not** + V

---

## 必要性與義務

### must — 強烈義務（說話者認為絕對必要）

- All participants **must** sign the consent form.
- You **must** not tamper with the samples.（mustn't = 絕對禁止）

### have to — 外部規定、客觀義務

- Researchers **have to** follow the ethical guidelines.（規定如此）
- She **doesn't have to** attend.（不需要，沒有義務）← 注意：don't have to ≠ mustn't

`mustn't` = 禁止做；`don't have to` = 不需要做（但可以做）

### should / ought to — 建議、應該（但不強制）

- Scientists **should** consider potential biases.
- You **ought to** review the methodology before proceeding.

## 可能性與推測

這是 TOEFL 閱讀最常出現的情態動詞用法，表達「對現在或過去的推測」。

### must — 幾乎確定（邏輯推斷）

- The temperature is dropping rapidly. There **must** be a system failure.（幾乎確定）

### may / might — 可能（不確定）

- The new treatment **may** reduce side effects.（可能，較正式）
- Further testing **might** reveal additional factors.（可能，較謹慎）

兩者意思相近，`might` 比 `may` 的確定程度稍低一點。

### could — 理論上的可能性

- This finding **could** have significant implications.
- The anomaly **could** be explained by several factors.

> 托福閱讀常用 `may / might / could` 來表達學術上的謹慎態度，不輕易斷言。

### can't / couldn't — 幾乎不可能

- That **can't** be the right explanation — the data contradict it.
- The sample size **couldn't** account for all the variation.

### 推測的強度排列

確定 → 不確定：**must** > **should** > **may / might** > **could**

---

## 過去的推測

情態動詞 + **have + p.p.** = 對過去的推測：

- The experiment **must have been** conducted under controlled conditions.（幾乎確定）
- The data **may have been** affected by external variables.（可能）
- They **could have made** an error in the calculation.（有可能犯了錯）
- She **should have submitted** the report earlier.（本來應該做，但沒做）
- He **would have noticed** the anomaly if he had checked.（假設語氣，如果…就會）

---

## will vs would

### will — 預測、承諾、事實

- The results **will** be published next month.
- If we increase the temperature, the reaction **will** accelerate.

### would — 假設、委婉、過去的習慣

- If I **were** a researcher, I **would** focus on genetics.（假設）
- **Would** you mind explaining the methodology?（委婉請求）
- As a child, she **would** spend hours in the library.（過去的習慣）

---

## 學術文章中的情態動詞

TOEFL 學術文章中，情態動詞常用來表達「謹慎態度」，避免過度絕對的陳述：

- ✗ This proves that... → ✓ This **may** suggest that...
- ✗ The results show... → ✓ The results **could** indicate...
- ✓ Future research **should** examine...
- ✓ This **might** have implications for...

看到這類表達，要理解作者刻意在保留不確定性，不是在說「一定」。
